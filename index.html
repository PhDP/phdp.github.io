I’m a postdoctoral reseacher working on Artificial Intelligence applied to disease ecology at the Poisot lab (University of Montreal). My Ph.D. focused on the automatic revision of ecological theories, which, in practice, means I spent a lot of time at the intersection of logic and probability theory. I’m particularly interested in how these techniques can bring safer, more transparent intelligent systems. I currently focus on probabilistic typed lambda-calculi and learning mathematical formulas from data.

I did my Ph.D. at the Canada Research Chair on Integrative ecology (Université de Sherbrooke). My work was supported by an Alexander Graham Bell Graduate Scholarship from the NSERC, a generous Azure for Research Award from Microsoft Research, and a hardware donation from NVIDIA.

My work relies on many high-quality open-source projects, including Glasgow Haskell Compiler, Rust, Lean Theorem Prover, Linux/Ubuntu, Vim/Neovim, Git, LLVM, GCC.
